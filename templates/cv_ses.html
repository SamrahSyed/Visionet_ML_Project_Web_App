Semantic Segmentation

Semantic segmentation is the process of partitioning a digital image into multiple segments (sets of pixels, also known as image objects). The goal of segmentation is to simplify and/or change the representation of an image into something that is more meaningful and easier to analyze. Unlike Instance Segmentation, Semantic Segmentation doesn't differ across different instances of the same object.
